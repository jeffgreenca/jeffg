## Jeff's Recommended Reading Short List #### Tech * How Complex Systems Fail * Nearly any profession with operational responsibility will benefit * https://www.adaptivecapacitylabs.com/HowComplexSystemsFail.pdf * Staff Engineer's Path * https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-staff-engineers/9781098118723/ * Code Complete, 2nd Edition * Great read for non-traditional background developers * https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/code-complete-2nd/0735619670/ * Designing Data-Intensive Applications * https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/designing-data-intensive-applications/9781491903063/ * Liars and Outliars * https://www.schneier.com/books/liars-and-outliers/ * IBM and the Holocaust * https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IBM_and_the_Holocaust #### Management * Managing Humans * Can provide great insight into management for non-managers. * https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/managing-humans-biting/9781430243144/ * The One Minute Manager Meets the Monkey * A short read on how to delegate. * https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/770565 #### DevOps * Google SRE Woorkbook * https://sre.google/workbook/table-of-contents/ * The DevOps Handbook * Worth a read - more concrete than the Phoenix Project * https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-devops-handbook/9781457191381/ #### Broader Topics * Difficult Conversations: How to Discuss What Matters Most * Framework for challenging professional and personal situations. * https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/774088 * Why Meditate: Working with Thoughts and Emotions * https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/8725111-why-meditate #### Fun Stuff * Every Tool's a Hammer: Life is What You Make It * I found Adam Savage's book surprisingly relevant to software engineering. * https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/43319933 * If I Understood You, Would I Have This Look On My Face? * Mix of fluff and content, but a fun read.
