## Jeff's Reading Shortlist #### Technical and Career * How Complex Systems Fail * Nearly any profession with operational responsibility will benefit * https://www.adaptivecapacitylabs.com/HowComplexSystemsFail.pdf * Staff Engineer's Path * https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-staff-engineers/9781098118723/ * Managing Humans * https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/managing-humans-biting/9781430243144/ * Code Complete, 2nd Edition * Great read for non-traditional background developers * https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/code-complete-2nd/0735619670/ #### Security * IBM and the Holocaust * https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IBM_and_the_Holocaust * Liars and Outliars * https://www.schneier.com/books/liars-and-outliers/ #### DevOps * Google SRE Woorkbook * https://sre.google/workbook/table-of-contents/ * The DevOps Handbook * Worth a read - more concrete than the Phoenix Project * https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-devops-handbook/9781457191381/ #### Broader Topics * Difficult Conversations: How to Discuss What Matters Most * Really great framework for challenging professional and personal situations. * https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/774088 * Why Meditate: Working with Thoughts and Emotions * https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/8725111-why-meditate #### Fun Stuff * Every Tool's a Hammer: Life is What You Make It * I found Adam Savage's book surprisingly relevant to software engineering * https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/43319933 * If I Understood You, Would I Have This Look On My Face? * A fair bit of fluff but a useful and fun read. Audiobook is narrated by Alan Alda himself. * https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/40600256
